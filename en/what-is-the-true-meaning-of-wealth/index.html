What is the True Meaning of Wealth?

ReachYL18 January, 2024
0 283 3 minutes read
Facebook Twitter LinkedIn Pinterest Reddit

Most people, when they mention “wealth“, understand that wealth means having a lot of money, valuable assets, etc. And they often have love or hate for wealth.

Some people feel angry towards those who have money, but spend their whole life trying to earn as much money as they do. The reason most people never achieve “wealth” is because they do not truly understand the true meaning of wealth.

So what is the true meaning of wealth?

1. “Wealthy” is when you no longer need to make an effort to earn money to sustain your daily life.

When you don’t need to go to work to make money from a job day in and day out, but still can live comfortably. To put it bluntly, you can go to bed without doing anything and still have money.

So where does the money come from?

The amount comes from the things that help you make money even when you sleep, meaning from your passive income sources.

Passive income sources can come from:

  • bank interest rates
  • advertising from videos on youtube, personal websites, …
  • selling digital products such as: audio, beats, ebooks, websites, …
  • investment items such as stock investment, digital currency, …

Those are the specific jobs that require direct involvement in order to earn money, such as being a wage laborer, an employee, etc. These are your active sources of income. These jobs require you to exchange your health and time for money. If you no longer do these jobs, then for sure you will have no more money.

But our time and health are limited, you can’t work forever, so if you want to become “rich,” you need to find activities, things that can help you generate passive income.

“Depending on the amount of money that makes you feel happy with life,
create passive income sources for yourself so that they always exceed that amount of expenses.”

Example:
+ Suppose you only need 15 million VND per month, then you can live comfortably and feel happy with life. Therefore, you need to create a passive income greater than 15 million VND per month, and then you will have found wealth for yourself.

+ If, for example, someone needs to spend 200 million per month to achieve the happiness they desire, then that person will need to generate a passive income greater than 200 million per month in order to become wealthy.

And an undeniable fact is that if you spend more than you earn, the only result you will achieve is debt and borrowing.

=> Being wealthy means that your passive income must exceed the amount you need to spend to help you feel happy with life.

2. Happiness is Wealth.

Ultimately, the desire for wealth is aimed at improving one’s quality of life, to alleviate worries about paying for electricity, rent, and daily expenses, to have the freedom to buy things one truly enjoys without constantly worrying about the cost, and to be able to provide the best for beloved family and friends.

=> To make your life more meaningful, happier.

You don’t have to become as wealthy as someone else, you don’t have to make as much money as they do to become rich. What you need is to know how much is enough to make you feel happy.

The rich and the successful, they all know their goals, they understand themselves, know what they truly like, and how much is enough for them.

Based on my interviews and research on wealthy individuals, there are many affluent people who lead a very simple lifestyle, and this accounts for over 80% of such individuals in the wealthy community. For example: Bill Gates, Mark Zuckerberg, Warren Buffett, Amancio Ortega, and so on.

But there are many people with not very high salaries who still want to buy luxury clothing, expensive handbags, spending on impulsive desires, therefore it almost does not bring value to the development but only makes you always in a state of lack of money, out of money.

I’m not saying that they are wealthy, they don’t use money, but one thing for sure is that they spend a lot, however, the amount they spend is always less than the amount of their passive income.

And especially, they do not spend money on things that they see as not helpful in their lives, desires that are impulsive, on the contrary, they will definitely spend generously on what they need, what they really like, what helps them develop themselves and increase their passive income stream.

By understanding their own lives, their personalities, and their tastes, they can minimize spending on unnecessary things and maximize spending on other essential needs to become more developed. That is one of the reasons why the rich get richer.

=> When you understand your own personality, your current life, and the life you desire in the future, that’s when you will find happiness, and that is also the wealth you desire.

*** Conclusion:

I think by now, you all understand what I am aiming for. Now, I believe each of you has a definition of “wealth” that you want to achieve in the future. It’s not about seeking the wealth of others, but about seeking your own wealth.
